Stage 01 — Approach

The vehicle enters Dwaar AI's detection zone.

As the vehicle turns toward the gate, all three identification systems activate simultaneously. RFID begins scanning for tags. The ANPR camera starts its feed. The facial detection engine watches the windscreen and surrounding area. Everything happens in parallel — there's no sequence, no waiting for one to fail before trying the next.

Stage 02 — RFID Identification

The vehicle's RFID tag is read. Matched. Gate opens.

A UHF RFID tag — typically a small sticker on the windscreen or bumper — is detected by the gate reader from 3 to 5 metres away. The tag ID is matched against the society's vehicle registry. If it's a registered vehicle, the boom barrier opens automatically. The entire process takes under one second. The driver never slows down.

Stage 03 — ANPR Plate Recognition

Camera reads the plate. System matches it. Simultaneously.

While RFID is scanning, an ANPR camera captures the vehicle's number plate. Dwaar AI's plate recognition engine reads the characters, normalises the text, and matches it against the vehicle registry. This happens in parallel — meaning for registered vehicles, RFID and ANPR both confirm the match. For vehicles without RFID tags, ANPR alone handles clearance.

Stage 04 — Facial Detection & OTP

Known persons — registered residents, domestic workers, regular staff — are identified and cleared. Unknown persons are shown on the guard's tablet with a prompt: request OTP from the visiting resident, or register as a new visitor.

Frequently asked

Questions we get asked before every demo.

UHF (Ultra High Frequency) RFID is a wireless identification technology. A small RFID tag — typically a windscreen sticker or bumper tag — is fitted to each registered vehicle. When the vehicle approaches the gate, a UHF reader detects the tag from 3 to 5 metres away. The tag ID is matched against the vehicle registry and if confirmed, the boom barrier opens automatically before the car reaches it. The driver never needs to stop, roll down a window, or use an app.

ANPR stands for Automatic Number Plate Recognition. A camera captures the vehicle's number plate as it approaches and Dwaar AI's recognition engine reads and matches it against the vehicle registry in under one second. Our model is specifically trained on Indian number plate formats — including BH-series plates, state codes, and older formats. It works in low light using IR-enabled cameras and functions at normal approach speeds without the vehicle needing to slow down.

Dwaar AI's proprietary AI model analyses the live gate camera feed. Faces are compared against a registered database of residents, staff and authorised visitors. Known persons are identified and cleared within a second. Unknown persons are flagged on the guard's tablet — the guard can trigger an OTP flow, request resident approval, or register the person as a new visitor. The AI processing runs locally at the gate hardware — it does not require a cloud round-trip for each identification, which means it works even on low-bandwidth connections.

The fallback chain is Dwaar AI's core architectural differentiator. All three modalities — RFID, ANPR and facial detection — run simultaneously, not in sequence. If RFID signal is unavailable, ANPR automatically handles identification. If ANPR cannot read the plate, face detection provides identification for known persons. If none can confirm automatically, the guard's tablet shows the event for manual action. This architecture means no single failure point can stop the gate from operating. Most other gate management platforms depend on a single technology or use third-party integrations that have no fallback logic.

The Guard App is a single-screen Android tablet application showing a live queue of all gate events. Automatically cleared entries appear with a green status. Events needing guard action — unknown visitors, OTP verification, override requests — appear highlighted with clear action buttons. The guard can approve, deny or register visitors in two taps. Manual gate controls are always accessible at the bottom of the screen. The app is available in Hindi and regional languages and requires no technical training.

The Admin Portal is a web-based dashboard for RWA committees, facility managers and security administrators. It provides real-time gate status across all entry points, a complete vehicle and resident registry, full event logs with timestamp, identification method and photo for every entry and exit, one-click exportable audit reports, gate configuration controls, and role-based access management. Multi-community and multi-gate deployments are managed from a single login. No software installation needed — it works in any web browser.
