The problem

Your gate is the first line of security. It shouldn't be the weakest link.

Most gate systems in India are either a paper register with an overworked guard, or an app that bolts hardware on as an afterthought. Neither was built to actually work.

The system goes down. The gate chaos begins.

App crashes, internet drops, hardware glitches — and suddenly your guard is back to a paper book and a phone call. Sound familiar? It's not a technology failure. It's a design failure.

Software and hardware that don't speak to each other.

Most platforms treat RFID and cameras as afterthoughts — third-party integrations stitched together. When one breaks, the whole system breaks. No fallback. No plan B.

Your data being sold back to you as advertising.

At least one major competitor uses your residents' data to run a marketplace and ad platform. Your community's entry records and contact details fund someone else's revenue model.

The solution

Three layers of intelligence.
One gate that never fails.

Dwaar AI is the only gate management platform built as a native hardware-software stack. RFID, ANPR, and AI facial detection run in parallel — not as integrations, but as one unified system. If one layer has a problem, the other two take over automatically.

1

UHF RFID — vehicles

Registered cars identified before they reach the barrier. Gate opens automatically. Zero stopping, zero delay.

2

ANPR — number plate recognition

Cameras read every licence plate in real time — day or night, at speed. Matched against your vehicle registry instantly.

3

AI facial detection — persons

Pedestrians, staff, and visitors identified at the gate. No RFID tag needed. No OTP to fumble with.

The fallback chain — Dwaar AI's architecture

RFID reads the vehicle

UHF tag detected 3–5 metres from barrier. Gate opens before the car stops.

If RFID isn't available — ANPR takes over

Camera reads the number plate. Matched in under a second. Gate still opens automatically.

If camera is obscured — face detection activates

AI identifies known persons. Unknown visitors get OTP verification via the guard tablet.

The gate never fails.

Three layers. Automatic fallback. Always a way through for the right person.

Common questions

Everything you need to know before you decide.

Dwaar AI is an AI-powered gate management system that combines three technologies into one unified platform: UHF RFID (for vehicles), ANPR number plate recognition (for all vehicles), and AI facial detection (for persons). All three run simultaneously and act as a fallback chain — if one layer fails, the others take over. The system includes a mobile app for residents, a tablet workstation for security guards, and a web-based admin portal for RWA committees and facility managers.

The core difference is architecture. MyGate and NoBrokerHood are app-first platforms where RFID and cameras are third-party add-ons — not built-in. Dwaar AI is a native hardware-software platform where all three modalities (RFID, ANPR, face detection) are engineered together. This means Dwaar AI has an automatic fallback chain — if RFID signal drops, ANPR takes over. Additionally, Dwaar AI has no advertising platform and does not resell resident data, which has been a reported concern with some competitors.

Dwaar AI is software-first and hardware-open. We partner with leading RFID reader and LPR camera hardware vendors. If you already have compatible hardware, we will assess whether it can integrate with our platform. If you need hardware, we'll recommend best-in-class devices from our partner network — without locking you into a single vendor.

Dwaar AI works wherever a barrier needs intelligent access control — not just housing societies. We serve gated residential communities, corporate offices, IT parks, mall parking, hospitals, hotels, warehouses, ports, factories, logistics hubs, schools, government buildings, stadiums, and fuel stations. Each vertical has a dedicated landing page on our website with industry-specific pain points and solutions.

This is exactly what the fallback chain is designed for. Dwaar AI's three modalities — RFID, ANPR, and face detection — run in parallel. If internet connectivity drops, local processing continues for RFID and ANPR decisions. Manual gate controls are always available on the guard's tablet as a final fallback. The gate never relies on a single point of failure.

This includes hardware installation by our partner team, software configuration, resident onboarding, and guard training.
