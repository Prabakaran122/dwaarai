Gate management for commercial

An IT park with 50 companies needs
one gate platform, not fifty policies.

Dwaar AI gives IT park operators a unified gate management platform — with per-company access zones, shift-based RFID entry, visitor compliance logs, and one dashboard for the park operator and each tenant company.

The problem

IT parks and SEZs have compliance requirements that standard gate systems can't meet.

Multiple tenant companies, shifting staff rosters, government visitor logs, and contractor zones — each with different access rules and different compliance requirements.

Multiple companies, multiple access rules — one entry point.

An IT park with 20 companies has 20 different policies for who can go where. Company A's employees should not be in Company B's server room. But with a single barrier and a guard, you're trusting one person to enforce twenty policies mentally.

"We had an employee from a competing tenant enter our development floor. Nobody stopped them at the gate. The guard just saw a badge." — IT Security Manager, Bengaluru tech park

Government SEZ compliance requires documented visitor records.

SEZ units must maintain visitor registers for government inspection. The log must include name, company, ID, host, entry time, and exit time. Paper registers don't just fail audits — they create legal exposure.

"Our SEZ unit was flagged during an inspection for incomplete visitor records. We had entries with no exit times recorded." — Compliance Officer, Chennai SEZ

Night shifts and weekend access have zero oversight.

Your day shift has a full guard team. Your 3am shift has one guard, fatigue, and no digital backup. Night and weekend entries are the highest risk period — and the period with the least accountability.

"A data incident traced back to a contractor entry at 2:30am on a Saturday. Our log had no photo, no plate, just a signature." — IT Park Security Head, Hyderabad
How Dwaar AI solves it

One platform. Built for every challenge your gate faces.

Per-company access zones — enforced by software, not guards.

Each company's registered employees are linked to their building zone. RFID or face detection enforces zone access at every gate point. Cross-zone entry attempts are flagged automatically.

SEZ-compliant visitor logs — photo, ID, host, timestamp.

Every visitor is registered with photo, company, host name, and purpose. OTP passes have built-in entry and exit logging. Export a compliant visitor register for any date range instantly.

Shift-based access — time policies enforced automatically.

Define access windows per employee, per company, or per zone. Night shift staff can only enter during their window. Weekend contractor access expires at Sunday midnight. No manual enforcement needed.

One dashboard for the park operator, per-company view for tenants.

The IT park operator sees all gates, all companies, all events. Each tenant company sees only their employees and visitors. One platform, appropriately scoped for every stakeholder.
