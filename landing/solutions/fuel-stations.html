The problem

Fuel stations deal with high vehicle throughput and specific accountability requirements.

Fleet billing, drive-off incidents, government fuel allocation compliance, and retail audit requirements all need vehicle identity records that manual systems can't reliably provide.

Drive-offs with no plate record — zero recovery.

A vehicle fills up and leaves without paying. Your attendant saw it happen but couldn't note the plate in time. With no ANPR, you have no evidence, no recourse, and no deterrent for next time.

"We had 8 drive-offs in 3 months. We caught none of them because we had no plate record. Our CCTV was too far away to read numbers." — Fuel Station Owner, Bangalore highway

Fleet customers require vehicle-linked billing — manual recording fails.

Corporate fleet accounts require accurate, vehicle-level billing records. If your attendant manually notes plates, errors are inevitable — and disputed invoices are time-consuming to resolve.

"A large fleet client disputed their monthly invoice because 12 of our manually noted plates didn't match their fleet list. We had to issue a credit." — Fuel Station Manager, Mumbai

Government fuel audit requires vehicle-level transaction records.

Subsidised fuel schemes, LPG allocation, and government fleet accounts all require documented vehicle-level dispensing records. Manual log books with handwritten entries don't satisfy government audit requirements.

"Our oil company audit flagged 18% of our records for illegible or missing vehicle numbers. We were put on a compliance improvement plan." — Petrol Pump Owner, Delhi NCR
How Dwaar AI solves it

One platform. Built for every challenge your gate faces.

ANPR logs every plate on entry — automatically, every vehicle.

Cameras read every vehicle plate as it enters the forecourt. Logged with timestamp, lane, and camera ID. No attendant input required. Clean, accurate records from the first vehicle of the day.

Fleet customer recognition — registered vehicles get priority service.

Corporate fleet accounts are registered by plate. When a fleet vehicle arrives, the system recognises it instantly. The attendant's terminal shows the account name, fuel limit, and billing reference.

Drive-off evidence — plate captured before and after fuelling.

Entry and exit ANPR captures the plate at both ends of the fuelling visit. In a drive-off incident, the complete sequence — vehicle arrival, time at pump, exit without payment — is logged with photos.

Compliance-ready audit log — every vehicle, every transaction.

Export a complete dispensing log — plate, entry time, fuel type, lane, exit time — for any date range. Ready for oil company audits, government fuel allocation reviews, and insurance claims.

Common questions

Questions from institutions decision-makers.

Dwaar AI installs ANPR cameras at the forecourt entry and exit points. Every vehicle plate is read and logged automatically on entry. At exit, the plate is read again, completing the visit record with entry time, exit time, and duration. No attendant input is required for plate logging. For fleet vehicles, the system matches the plate to the registered account and displays the account details on the attendant terminal.

ANPR captures the vehicle plate on entry — before fuelling begins. A second ANPR read on exit closes the visit record. If a vehicle exits without completing payment, the system flags the event. The complete evidence package — entry plate photo, exit plate photo, timestamps, pump lane, and fuel amount — is available immediately for police FIR or insurance documentation. The ANPR record also serves as a deterrent, as most drive-off prevention relies on visible plate-reading cameras.

Corporate fleet accounts are registered in Dwaar AI with their vehicle plates and billing details. When a fleet vehicle arrives, the ANPR system identifies the plate and displays the account name, approved fuel type, and any applicable limits on the attendant's terminal. Fuelling transactions are automatically linked to the account. Monthly billing reports per fleet account are generated from the admin portal in one click.
