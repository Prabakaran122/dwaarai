The problem

Government building access control has accountability requirements that other sectors don't.

RTI compliance, audit inspections, security clearance requirements, and VIP protocol — each demands a level of documentation that paper registers cannot provide.

Visitor logs that don't meet government audit standards.

Government facilities are subject to inspection by audit bodies, security agencies, and parliamentary committees. A visitor register with handwritten names and no ID verification, no photo, and no exit time creates audit exposure and potential RTI liability.

"Our facility audit flagged 23% of visitor entries as incomplete — missing ID numbers, no exit recorded, or illegible handwriting." — Security Officer, Central Government Office, Delhi

No face verification for officers and staff — proxy attendance possible.

Without biometric or face-verified entry, government staff attendance can be proxied. This affects payroll, accountability, and security — especially in sensitive facilities where individual-level entry accountability is required.

"We found through a separate audit that 4 staff members had signed in colleagues who were not present." — Administrative Officer, State Secretariat

VIP and official vehicles delayed by standard gate procedure.

Ministers, secretaries, and senior officials should not queue at a barrier. But without automatic VIP identification, standard gate procedure applies to everyone — and one slow clearance during a protocol event creates a visible failure.

"A visiting Secretary was delayed at our entrance for 90 seconds while the guard located their vehicle on the expected list." — Protocol Officer, Government Complex
How Dwaar AI solves it

One platform. Built for every challenge your gate faces.

Visitor ID logging — government-standard documentation.

Every visitor is registered with name, ID number (Aadhaar, passport, or employee card), host officer, purpose, and entry/exit time. Photo captured on every visit. Exportable for RTI, audit, or security inspection.

All officers and staff face-verified — proxy entry eliminated.

Face recognition verifies every officer, staff member, and peon on every entry. Attendance is linked to gate entry data. No proxy possible. Shift and duty records accurate.

VIP vehicle priority — instant clearance for authorised vehicles.

Ministerial, official, and protocol vehicles are registered with priority flags. RFID or ANPR identification triggers immediate barrier opening — no guard confirmation required.

Audit-compliant records — always ready for inspection.

Every entry log is timestamped, photographed, and stored permanently. Export in PDF or CSV for government audit, security inspection, or internal compliance review — any date range, one click.

Common questions

Questions from institutions decision-makers.

Dwaar AI logs every visitor with their government-issued ID number, name, host officer, purpose of visit, and entry and exit times — all with a photo captured at the gate. These records are stored permanently and are exportable as a formatted PDF or CSV for any date range. The log format satisfies RTI compliance, government audit requirements, and security inspection documentation standards.

All permanent officers, contract staff, security personnel, and support staff are registered with a face photo in Dwaar AI's system. On every gate entry, the facial detection engine identifies each person and logs the entry with timestamp. Attendance records are automatically generated from gate entry data. Proxy entry — one person checking in for another — is not possible with face verification.

Ministerial, official, and protocol vehicles are registered in Dwaar AI with a priority flag. When the vehicle approaches the gate, RFID or ANPR identification triggers an immediate barrier open — no guard confirmation step required. The protocol officer or security post receives a notification simultaneously. The entire sequence from detection to open takes under 5 seconds.
