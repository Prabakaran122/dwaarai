The problem

Manual gate logs are a liability at scale.

A warehouse processing 200 trucks a day cannot rely on a guard with a clipboard. The errors, the delays, and the compliance gaps compound every single day.

Truck queues at peak hours cost you hours of dwell time.

10 trucks waiting while the guard writes down plate numbers, checks a printed list, and calls the dock manager. At 5 minutes per truck, that's 50 minutes of dwell time wasted every peak session. Those are trucks you're paying detention charges on.

"We were paying detention charges on 3–4 trucks a week because they sat at the gate for over an hour. The guard's log showed 40 entries written in 2 hours." — Warehouse Manager, JNPT logistics hub

Driver identity not verified — wrong person in a secure dock.

The truck is on the approved list. But is the driver the one who's registered? Without face verification, a truck plate match tells you the vehicle is right but not whether the driver belongs to the fleet that plate is registered to.

"A driver used a colleague's truck to access our pharma cold storage. He wasn't on the authorised list. We had no way to know." — Security Head, Mumbai pharma warehouse

Compliance audits require logs your current system can't produce.

ISO, FSSAI, pharma GDP, customs compliance — all require documented, timestamped vehicle entry records. Paper logs with handwritten plates and illegible signatures don't satisfy an auditor.

"Our GDP audit for the pharma client failed partly on vehicle entry records. The log had no timestamps and 3 plate numbers that were illegible." — Compliance Officer, cold chain logistics provider
How Dwaar AI solves it

One platform. Built for every challenge your gate faces.

ANPR logs every truck on entry and exit — automatically.

Cameras read every plate as vehicles approach the boom barrier. Matched against the approved vehicle list in under a second. No guard manual entry, no clipboard, no illegible writing.

Driver verified by face or OTP on every visit.

Registered drivers are face-identified at the gate. New or substitute drivers present an OTP from the fleet manager. Every entry links a driver identity to a plate — not just a plate.

Multi-zone dock control — truck goes to the right bay.

Different zones have different access rules. Zone A for inbound. Zone B for outbound. Cold storage zone restricted to approved cold-chain vehicles. Access enforced by ANPR, not a guard waving a flag.

Compliance reports generated automatically.

ISO, GDP, FSSAI or customs audit? Export a complete vehicle entry log — plate, driver, timestamp, dock zone, entry and exit — for any date range. One click. Clean data.

Common questions

Questions from industrial decision-makers.

Dwaar AI installs ANPR cameras at the warehouse gate. As a truck approaches, the camera reads the number plate in under 2 seconds. The plate is matched against the approved fleet list. If matched, the boom barrier opens. If not matched, the guard tablet shows an alert and the driver is directed to manual verification. The entire sequence is logged with timestamp, photo, and clearance method.

Dwaar AI adds a driver identity layer on top of plate recognition. Registered drivers are verified by face detection at the gate. For new or substitute drivers, the fleet dispatcher issues an OTP that the driver presents to the guard. Every entry log links both a vehicle plate and a verified driver identity — not just a plate match.

Dwaar AI generates a complete vehicle entry log for every gate event — plate number, driver identity, entry time, exit time, dock zone, identification method, and photo. These logs are stored in the admin portal and can be exported as PDF or CSV for any date range. This satisfies vehicle entry documentation requirements for ISO 9001, pharma GDP, FSSAI food safety audits, and customs compliance inspections.
