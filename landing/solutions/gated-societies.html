The problem

Your current gate system is working against you.

Most society gates in India still rely on a paper register, a guard with a phone, and software that bolts hardware on as an afterthought. Every layer is a bottleneck.

Paper registers that prove nothing.

Visitor names written by hand. No photo, no verification, no way to know if the person who signed is the person who entered. When something goes wrong, the register tells you nothing useful.

"Our guard asked every visitor to sign a book for 8 years. We had no idea if any of it was accurate." — RWA Secretary, Bengaluru

Gate queues every morning and evening.

Peak hours mean every vehicle stops while the guard calls the flat, waits for approval, writes down the number. 10 cars waiting. Residents frustrated. Staff clock in late. The bottleneck is built into the system.

"The morning queue was 15 cars by 9am every day. Residents were honking at each other." — Facility Manager, Mumbai

App that crashes when you need it most.

Internet goes down. Server is overloaded. App update fails. And suddenly your entire gate security is back to a guard making judgment calls without any information. One failure takes down everything.

"We had an outage on Diwali night. 200 families couldn't get their guests in. The guard just let everyone through." — Committee Member, Delhi NCR
How Dwaar AI solves it

Three problems. One platform. Zero single points of failure.

Every vehicle identified before it stops.

UHF RFID reads registered cars from 3–5 metres. The gate opens before the car reaches the barrier. Zero stopping, zero guard call, zero queue at peak hours.

Domestic staff and visitors — no OTP fumbling.

Registered maids, drivers and cooks are face-verified automatically every morning. Visitors get a digital pass from the resident. The guard just confirms — no phone calls needed.

Three fallback layers — gate never fails.

If RFID drops, ANPR reads the plate. If the camera is obscured, RFID still clears. All three run in parallel. One failure never stops the gate. Ever.

Full audit trail — every entry logged with photo.

Every vehicle, visitor and staff entry is timestamped with a photo snapshot. The RWA committee can pull reports for any date range in one click from the admin dashboard.

Common questions

Everything your RWA committee needs to know.

A gate management system for housing societies automates the identification and logging of every vehicle and person entering or exiting a gated residential complex. Dwaar AI's system combines UHF RFID for registered vehicles, ANPR for number plate reading, and AI facial detection for residents, staff and visitors — replacing paper registers, guard phone calls and manual approval processes.

Domestic workers — maids, cooks, drivers, security staff — are registered once with a face photo and assigned to the flats they work in. From the next visit onwards, the AI facial detection engine identifies them automatically at the gate. No RFID tag, no OTP, no guard check needed. Residents receive a push notification when their staff member enters. If a domestic worker's access is revoked by the admin, they cannot enter from the next gate scan.

Yes. From the Dwaar AI Resident App, you can create a digital visitor pass — a 6-digit OTP — and share it with your guest via WhatsApp or SMS before they arrive. When the guest reaches the gate, they show the OTP to the security guard, who verifies it on their tablet. The guard sees your name, flat number, and the pass validity period. If an unregistered visitor arrives without a pass, you receive a push notification with the visitor's photo and can approve or deny in one tap.

The fundamental difference is architecture. MyGate and NoBrokerHood are app-first platforms where RFID and cameras are third-party add-ons with no fallback logic — if the app is down or hardware integration fails, the gate stops working. Dwaar AI is a native hardware-software platform with RFID, ANPR and face detection all built in with an automatic fallback chain. Additionally, Dwaar AI has no advertising platform and does not use resident data for marketing — both have been reported concerns with some competitors.

A typical gated housing society is live within 30 days of contract signing. The process involves: hardware installation at each gate point (RFID readers, ANPR cameras, facial detection cameras, boom barrier integration) by our certified hardware partner team; platform configuration with your vehicle and resident database; resident app onboarding using unique invite codes; and guard training on the tablet app — which typically takes one session of under 2 hours. Multi-gate and large township deployments may take 45 days.
