The problem

Hospital gates serve too many different people, moving at very different urgencies.

Ambulances, ICU staff, visitors, vendors, and contractors all use the same gate. A system that treats them the same is a system that fails everyone.

Ambulances delayed by standard gate procedure.

An ambulance carrying a critical patient should never wait at a gate. But if the guard doesn't recognise the vehicle, or the system requires a manual check, those seconds are gone. One missed protocol and the delay is permanent.

"Our emergency gate protocol required 2 people to confirm an ambulance entry. It took 90 seconds on a bad night." — Hospital Administrator, Chennai

Visitor management is manual — overstaying visits go unnoticed.

Hospitals need to control who is in patient areas, for how long, and when. Paper passes have no end time. Someone who was supposed to leave at 7pm is still in the ICU corridor at 10pm. No system knows.

"A family dispute escalated in our ICU corridor because a visitor had overstayed by 4 hours. We had no record of when they entered." — Security Head, Mumbai hospital

Clinical staff rotation means guards don't recognise everyone.

Teaching hospitals and large facilities have hundreds of rotating doctors, interns and nurses. Guards can't know every face. New staff get stopped, questioned, delayed — at the start of every shift.

"Our interns were being stopped at the gate every day for the first 3 weeks. The guard rotation meant nobody recognised them." — Medical Superintendent, Bengaluru
How Dwaar AI solves it

One platform. Built for every challenge your gate faces.

Ambulance and emergency vehicle priority — instant clearance.

Registered emergency vehicles are flagged with priority status. RFID or ANPR identification triggers immediate barrier opening — no guard confirmation needed. Under 5 seconds from detection to clear.

All clinical staff face-verified — zero shift delay.

Doctors, nurses, interns and support staff are registered once. Face detection clears them on every subsequent shift entry — no badge, no OTP, no stopping at the gate.

Visitor time windows — entry and overstay control.

Visitor passes are issued with defined time windows. Overstay alerts trigger on the admin dashboard and guard tablet. Every visitor entry and exit is logged with photo and timestamp.

NABH-ready compliance logs — one-click export.

Every gate event meets NABH documentation requirements — timestamped, photographed, and categorised. Export for any date range. Ready for accreditation audits.

Common questions

Questions from commercial decision-makers.

Registered ambulances and emergency vehicles are given a priority flag in Dwaar AI. When identified by RFID or ANPR at the gate, the system immediately signals the boom barrier to open — no guard confirmation step required. The event is logged with timestamp, vehicle ID, and clearance method. The entire sequence from detection to open takes under 5 seconds.

Visitors are registered at the hospital reception desk. A digital OTP pass is issued with a specific time window — for example, 11am to 1pm for Ward B. The visitor shows the OTP at the gate. On the admin dashboard, the security team sees all active visitor passes and receives alerts for any overstay. Every visitor entry and exit is logged with photo and timestamp for NABH compliance.

NABH requires documented visitor management, staff entry records, and emergency access logs. Dwaar AI logs every gate event with a timestamp, photo, identification method, and clearance status. These records are stored in the admin portal and exportable as PDF or CSV for any date range — covering the documentation requirements for NABH, JCI, and internal accreditation audits.
