The problem

Port gate management at scale breaks paper-based systems completely.

Hundreds of trucks per shift, multi-zone restricted areas, customs compliance, and real-time violation alerts — paper logs and manual checks don't come close.

High-volume truck traffic — manual logging creates backlogs immediately.

A busy container terminal sees 500+ trucks in a single shift. Manual plate logging, document checking, and zone routing at a guard post creates backlogs in minutes. Dwell time climbs. Truckers queue for hours.

"Our gate team was the bottleneck for the entire terminal. Trucks were waiting 45 minutes to enter during shift change." — Terminal Manager, JNPT

Multi-zone compliance — wrong truck in the wrong zone is a customs incident.

Container terminal zones are restricted for customs, quarantine, and security reasons. A truck accessing the wrong zone isn't just an operational error — it can be a customs violation with significant penalties.

"A driver took a wrong turn and entered a customs-bonded zone. The customs officer held up 8 trucks while they investigated." — Operations Director, Chennai port

Customs and port authority audits require complete, timestamped vehicle records.

APSEZ, JNPT, and ICD auditors require documented vehicle entry logs — plate, driver, entry time, exit time, zone accessed, clearance method. Paper logs fail on every requirement.

"Our last customs audit showed 14% of entry records with missing or unreadable plate numbers. We received a formal notice." — Compliance Manager, Gujarat port
How Dwaar AI solves it

One platform. Built for every challenge your gate faces.

ANPR handles high-volume truck entry — no backlogs.

Multi-lane ANPR cameras log plates at full truck approach speed. No stopping required for recognised vehicles. Backlogs at the gate gate are replaced by a 2-second clearance per vehicle.

Multi-zone access — trucks routed to correct zones automatically.

Each vehicle is cleared only to its approved zone. Cross-zone access is blocked and flagged immediately. Guard tablets show routing instructions per vehicle. Wrong-zone entries become impossible for registered fleet.

Instant violation alerts — perimeter and zone breaches notified in real time.

ANPR monitors all zone boundaries. Unregistered vehicle in a restricted zone triggers an instant alert to the security control room and admin portal.

Customs-ready audit logs — complete, timestamped, exportable.

Every vehicle entry log includes plate, driver identity, entry time, exit time, zone accessed, and photo. Exportable for customs inspection, port authority audit, or APSEZ/ICD compliance requirements.

Common questions

Questions from industrial decision-makers.

Dwaar AI's ANPR cameras are deployed in multi-lane configurations at port entry gates. Cameras read truck and trailer plates at normal approach speed — no stopping required. Recognised vehicles in the approved fleet are cleared automatically. Unrecognised plates trigger a guard action alert. The system can handle multiple simultaneous lane entries without creating queue backlogs.

Each vehicle in Dwaar AI is assigned to one or more approved zones — for example, an inbound truck is cleared for Zone A unloading yard but not for the customs-bonded Zone C. If a vehicle attempts access to an unapproved zone, the barrier stays closed and the guard tablet shows a zone violation alert. Routing instructions for approved vehicles are displayed on the guard tablet.

Dwaar AI generates a complete vehicle movement log — plate, driver identity, entry gate, entry time, zone accessed, exit time, dwell duration, and clearance photo. These logs satisfy documentation requirements for customs compliance, APSEZ/JNPT port authority audits, and ICD inspection requirements. Reports are exportable as PDF or CSV from the admin portal in one click.
